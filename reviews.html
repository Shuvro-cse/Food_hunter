Name: Truffle Carbonara
Price: $28.00
Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
Location: L’Oro di Napoli, Downtown

Experience • Taste • Opinion

This isn't your average pasta. The guanciale is rendered to a perfect glass-like crisp, and the pecorino-to-egg ratio creates a silkiness that’s hard to find outside of Rome. The truffle is fresh, not oil-based, which makes the price tag feel justified. It’s a "special occasion" plate that actually delivers.

Name: Birria Quesatacos (Set of 3)
Price: $12.50
Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Location: Taco Truck "La Bamba", 5th & Main

Experience • Taste • Opinion

The consommé is the star here—deep, fatty, and rich with dried chili flavor. My only gripe? The wait time was 40 minutes on a Tuesday night, and the napkins provided didn't stand a chance against the grease. It’s messy, loud, and absolutely essential for any taco lover in the city.

Name: Pistachio Baklava Cheesecake
Price: $9.00
Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
Location: Zelda’s Mediterranean Bakery

Experience • Taste • Opinion

This is a fusion that shouldn't work, but it’s the best thing I’ve eaten all month. The honey-soaked phyllo base stays crunchy against the dense, tangy cheesecake filling. It’s heavy, so share it with a friend. My only "warning" is that they sell out by 2:00 PM every single day.
